Strobe rate sets the lock frequency of the inner ring (which has 2 segment-pairs — B/W/B/W). The disk internally rotates at half this rate, matching the classic Peterson/Stroboconn arrangement (the original Conn Stroboconn drove its A disk at 1650 RPM = 27.5 Hz from a 55 Hz signal). Outer rings (4, 8, 16 pairs) lock at 2×, 4×, 8× the strobe rate, so a single disk shows the fundamental and the next three octaves at once.

Pitch reference: A4 = 440 Hz, equal temperament (semitone ratio 21/12). If another tuner reads differently note-for-note, check whether it uses a non-440 reference (e.g. 442 Hz orchestral or 432 Hz alternative); that's a constant cent offset, not a per-note error.

Picking an audio source (microphone or tab audio) replaces the synthetic sine with real-time samples; for tab audio, choose a tab in the share dialog and check “Share tab audio”.
